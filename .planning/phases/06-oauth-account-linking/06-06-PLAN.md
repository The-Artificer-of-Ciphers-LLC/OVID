<objective>
Finish the routes.py security hardening: drop the Apple ES256 client-secret `exp` from six months to
~300s (still regenerated per token exchange — that IS AUTH-03's automated rotation, D-10), split the
IndieAuth routes onto their own `indieauth_router` registered conditionally on `OVID_ENABLE_INDIEAUTH`
(default off → 404, D-08), and derive the IndieAuth localhost bypass from `config.ALLOW_LOCALHOST_BYPASS`
(from Plan 03) instead of the hardcoded literal — making the bypass provably unreachable under
`OVID_ENV=production` (AUTH-10, D-09).

This plan runs after Plan 05 (routes.py exclusivity) and after Plan 03 (which defines
`config.ALLOW_LOCALHOST_BYPASS` and OVID_ENV). Two small, unrelated security concerns (AUTH-03 crypto
lifetime, AUTH-10 config-drift) are grouped here because both are minimal routes.py/main.py edits that
must serialize on routes.py after Plan 05.

Purpose: shrink the Apple secret exposure window and shrink the default auth surface to exactly the
four headline providers while proving the dev bypass cannot reach production.
Output: Apple exp change + test; IndieAuth router split + gating + localhost-bypass derivation + tests.
</objective>
